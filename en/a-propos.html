ABOUT

The Matilda Effect – Jocelyn Bell Burnell project was born at the MMi department of Montbéliard in 2025. Created as part of an educational exercise, it aims to pay tribute to invisibilized women scientists, starting with Jocelyn Bell Burnell, discoverer of pulsars, long erased from the official history of astronomy.

Our vision

Our team wanted to design a site that is both educational and immersive, blending science, culture, and social engagement. We believe it is possible to popularize without simplifying, to move while informing, and to speak of equality without aggressive activism simply by making truth visible.

The project's pillars

Ethical and eco-designed

With a sober website respectful of the digital environment.

Inclusive

By making science accessible to all, in French and English.

Collaborative

By mixing human work and intelligent assistance (AI) to enrich reflection and creation.

"Because every discovery deserves to have a name,
And every name deserves to be heard."
